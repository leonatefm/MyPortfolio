    Victors Experience

    Web Designer & Developer, University of Michigan

    (Content Under Construction)

    Victors Experience site is a showcase of the amazing work being done by Michigan engineers. The target users are alumni who may be interested in those projects and are willing to give future donations to help engineers to make our world better. Since we are called to attract donations, the website must look fancy. Thus, it actually quite challenging when you design for money.

    This website is created mainly by two people. We designed it together and for the development, I’m primarily responsible for the front end thing while my colleague focusing on the back end developing. The most challenging part of this project for me is the design of the transition among different views. As you may notice, this website is a single page application. So all the transitions are enabled using javascript in the client side. The data is loaded using AJAX and deployed manually. Since the information of each item is the same in all the views, so the HTML structure actually stays the same while the css style changes a lot when you switch the view.

    One common issue of one-page website is the loss of go back and forward feature. Here I use the hash in the url to track the interaction history and fake a natural feature of going back or forward. It’s a pain, but we made it.

To be an empathetic designer, a creative engineer, an insightful photographer and an inspiring storyteller.

I was born in the southeast coastal area of China, in a small, quite and beautiful town. But I was not a quite kid, in the reverse, I was always the noisy one with tons of questions and expressions. Fortunately, that curiosity has led me to many delightful advantages in my life. Delving into interaction design, is definitely one of them.

An occasional TED talk, The Sixth Sense, opened the door of a brand new world for me, a field called Human Computer Interaction. I was so amazed and inspired by that innovative connection they created between the physical and digital world. And that impulse soon evolved into my desire to become a professional in this area, pushed me to set foot on the soil of US and pursue the HCI Master degree in University of Michigan.

And now, my curiosity has brought me to IBM, to witness the inspirational journey of this big blue giant to adopt design thinking and transform business outcomes throughout the company. Currently, I'm a front end engineer in IBM Watson Education team, working at the intersection of artificial intelligence, cloud computing and human-centered design, aiming to revolutionize the learning experience through personalization.

With the goal of innovating the way human interacts with digital information, I'm eager to be involved with any freelance projects, opportunities & collaborations. Feel free to drop a message if you wanna talk.
